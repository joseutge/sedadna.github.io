The sedimentary ancient DNA (sedaDNA) has become a powerful tool in paleoecology and is now largely used to track past long-term changes in terrestrial and aquatic biodiversity. The sedaDNA scientific society aims to transmit information about current sedaDNA research, promote best practices and increase collaborations between research groups.
